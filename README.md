# Hello 🌍! 

I’m **Amgad**,  a CS graduate student at TUM and UC Berkeley, building tech that move the world forward.

Life speedrun: Trading cards at 7, selling door-to-door at 10, candy sales from schoolbag at 12. At 16, I was flipping cars in Dubai and scaled to luxury imports from Oman. Many people didn't think much of me early on, and I gave them plenty of reasons to. I didn't take school seriously until I realized talent without discipline is wasted potential. Once I treated learning like a system to optimize, everything changed. That led to CS and research at TUM & UC Berkeley, and Amazon, where I was recognized as one of Europe's top grad students for simplifying complex problems.

My research at Berkeley and TUM spans from spatial computing to how algorithmic objectives, optimizing for engagement versus credibility, shape public perception. I love doing research and it's great to explore curiosity, but building something in the real world answers it. It's become clear to me that in this era, the market teaches faster than any study. That pushed me away from the PhD path and toward building.

I am writing my own story. I always seek to build ambitious products, be known for my craft and use my leverage to create real opportunities for people who deserve it.

If you're building something interesting or just want to chat, feel free to reach out.
