# Hello 🌍! 

I’m **Amgad**,  a CS graduate student at TUM and UC Berkeley, building tech that move the world forward.

Life speedrun: Trading cards at 7, selling door-to-door at 10, candy sales from schoolbag at 12. As a teenager I was importing cars from Oman into the UAE and flipping them for profit. To gain an edge, I coded simple market watchers and this became my bridge into computer science and machine learning at TUM & UC Berkeley, and roles at companies like Amazon, where I became focused on technology as a gateway to building and scaling anything. I didn't take school seriously until I realized talent without discipline is wasted potential. Once I treated learning like a system to optimize and break down into smaller pieces, everything changed and lead to stuff like being recognized as one of Europe's top grad students at Amazon for simplifying complex problems.

My research at Berkeley and TUM spans from wokring with CNNs on biometric data to how algorithmic objectives, optimizing for engagement versus credibility, shape public perception. I love doing research and it's great to explore curiosity, but I feel that building something in the real world more so answers it. It's become clear to me that in this era, the market teaches faster than any study and that pushed me away from the PhD path and toward building.

I am writing my own story and I always seek to build ambitious products, be known for my craft and use my leverage to create real opportunities for others.

If you're building something interesting or just want to chat, feel free to reach out.
